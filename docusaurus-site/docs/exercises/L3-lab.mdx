## Problem 3.1

Let $\succ$ be a Knuth–Bendix ordering (KBO) with precedence $\text{inverse} \gg \text{times}$. For the literal

$$
\text{inverse}(\text{times}(x,y)) = \text{times}(\text{inverse}(y), \text{inverse}(x)),
$$

compare the left- and right-hand side terms under $\succ$ in the following cases:

1. $\text{weight}(\text{inverse}) = \text{weight}(\text{times}) = 1$
2. $\text{weight}(\text{inverse}) = 0$ and $\text{weight}(\text{times}) = 1$

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

1. Both sides have weights $2 + \text{weight}(x) + \text{weight}(y)$ on the left, and $3 + \text{weight}(y) + \text{weight}(x)$ on the right. Hence $\text{times}(\text{inverse}(y), \text{inverse}(x)) \succ \text{inverse}(\text{times}(x,y))$.
2. Both sides have weight $1 + \text{weight}(x) + \text{weight}(y)$. Tie-breaking by precedence gives $\text{inverse} \gg \text{times}$, so $\text{inverse}(\text{times}(x,y)) \succ \text{times}(\text{inverse}(y), \text{inverse}(x))$.

</details>


## Problem 3.2

Let $\Sigma$ be a signature containing only function symbols and at least one constant. Let $\gg$ be a precedence on $\Sigma$ and $w: \Sigma \to \mathbb{N}$ a weight function compatible with $\gg$. Describe the ground terms of minimal weight with respect to the KBO induced by $\gg$ and $w$.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

Minimal-weight ground terms are either:

- A constant $c \in \Sigma$ of minimal weight among the constants, or
- A term $f^n(c)$ (with $n \ge 1$) where $w(f) = 0$ and $c$ is a minimal-weight constant.

</details>


## Problem 3.3

Consider the ground clause set

$$
\begin{aligned}
(1)\; & g(f(a)) = a \lor g(f(b)) = a \\
(2)\; & f(a) = a \\
(3)\; & f(b) \neq f(b) \lor f(b) = a \\
(4)\; & g(a) \neq a
\end{aligned}
$$

Show that $S = \{(1),(2),(3),(4)\}$ is unsatisfiable by saturating with the ground superposition calculus $\mathbb{SUP}_{\succ,\sigma}$ (including ground binary resolution) using a well-behaved selection function $\sigma$ under:

1. $\succ$ generated by $f \gg a \gg g \gg b$ with weights $w(f)=0$, $w(a)=2$, $w(g)=3$, $w(b)=1$.
2. $\succ$ generated by $g \gg a \gg b \gg f$ with weights $w(g)=0$, $w(a)=3$, $w(f)=1$, $w(b)=1$.

State which literals are selected and the maximal terms.

<details>
<summary>Solution</summary>

Label the clauses $(1)$–$(4)$ as above; selected literals are underlined.

1. Ordering $f \gg a \gg g \gg b$.
   - $(1)\; \underline{g(f(a)) = a} \lor g(f(b)) = a$ because $\{g(f(a)),a\} \succ \{g(f(b)),a\}$.
   - $(2)\; \underline{f(a) = a}$ (only literal).
   - $(3)\; \underline{f(b) \neq f(b)} \lor f(b) = a$ (negative literal selected).
   - $(4)\; \underline{g(a) \neq a}$ (only literal).
   - Equality resolution on $(3)$ yields $(5)\; \underline{f(b) = a}$.
   - Superposition of $(2)$ into $(1)$ gives $(6)\; \underline{g(a) = a} \lor g(f(b)) = a$ with $g(a)$ maximal.
   - Resolving $(6)$ with $(4)$ yields $(7)\; \underline{g(f(b)) = a}$.
   - Superposition of $(4)$ into $(5)$ produces $(8)\; \underline{g(f(b)) \neq a}$.
   - Resolving $(7)$ and $(8)$ gives $\square$.

2. Ordering $g \gg a \gg b \gg f$ yields the same literal comparisons (by weight or precedence), so the derivation repeats verbatim and again produces $\square$.

</details>
